# Street Chronicles: Biome Narrative Outline

## Core Premise

> **Operative Gleaner** (Homeland Security / BPRD) is sent to a small Pacific Northwest town to investigate dragon containment breaches. The "hero" is **The Seeker** — a Chinese MSS operative who has discovered the truth: the pandas are chimeras, a Trojan horse designed to harvest organs for Western vampires (The Pale Court). As outbreaks occur, Gleaner must choose between their handler's orders and the truth.

---

## Faction Overview

### 1. Chinese MSS (Ministry of State Security)

**Goal**: Protect dragons and contain the "dragon event" in the West
**Motivation**: Believe pandas are sacred guardians, unaware they're a trojan horse
**Play**: NPCs friendly to player initially, becomes conflicted as truth emerges

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Operative | **The Seeker** (The Hero) — seeking answers about outbreaks |
| Objective | Contain dragon events, protect what they believe is sacred |
| Blind spot | Panda chimera = Trojan horse for vampire organ harvest |
| Arc | Discovers truth → ally or enemy depending on player choice |

---

### 2. Pinkertons (Private Detective Agency)

**Goal**: Take down the Chinese Empire through a 6,000-year-old blood feud
**Motivation**: Ancient grudge against Eastern dynasties
**Play**: Antagonists who are "accidentally" helping expose the truth

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Method | Baiting Chinese around with "dragon snuff" — waking up dragon secrets |
| Effect | Each dragon event reveals more panda → trojan horse connection |
| Arc | Unwitting allies; their sabotage inadvertently aids The Seeker's discovery |
| Conflict | Hates vampires too but won't work with Chinese to stop them |

---

### 3. The Jesuits (Hidden Order)

**Goal**: Protect the chimera secret indefinitely
**Motivation**: Created the panda chimeras 400 years ago; must maintain cover
**Play**: Behind-the-scenes antagonists, supply quest hints

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Origin | Gifted first panda to Chinese imperial court in 1624 |
| Secret | Pandas are engineered chimeras for growing human-compatible organs |
| Goal | Prevent anyone from connecting pandas to vampire longevity |
| Arc | Deploy obstacles; will eliminate players who get too close to truth |

---

### 4. Homeland Security / BPRD (Bureau for Paranormal Research and Defense)

**Goal**: Protect the chimera secret while deploying Operative Gleaner
**Motivation**: Mainstream world must never know about paranormal elements
**Play**: Player's employer; eventual moral choice point

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Operative | **Operative Gleaner** (The Player) — handler-selected hunter |
| Assignment | Investigate small-town dragon containment breach |
| Agenda | Contain truth, not necessarily eliminate The Seeker |
| Arc | Discovers handler's true agenda → defect or comply? |

---

## The Panda Chimera (Central MacGuffin)

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Origin | Jesuit scientists, 1624 — gifted to Chinese Emperor |
| Purpose | Grow human-compatible organs in chimera hosts |
| Harvest | Organs extend vampire life indefinitely |
| Scale | Every panda in the world is a living organ factory |
| Reveal | Dragon events = distress signals when harvest cycles fail |

## Story Arcs

### Act 1: The Job (Stardew Valley pacing)

**Tone:** Chores and light combat. The player is a janitor learning the ropes. The conspiracy is environmental wallpaper: lore books, ambient barks, overheard fragments. No faction contact is hostile. The world is small and safe.

**Floors:** 0 (The Approach), 1 (The Promenade), 1.1-1.6 (interiors), 2 (Lantern Row), 2.1-2.2 (interiors), 2.2.1-2.2.2 (dungeons)

| Beat | Location | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| Opening | Floor 0 → 1 | Arrive at post. Dispatcher grab. Get work keys from home. |
| First dungeon | Floor 1.3.1 | Soft Cellar tutorial. Clean, restock, light combat. |
| Hero's Wake | Floor 2.2.1 | Foyer encounter: witness the Hero mid-slaughter. "You are not the hero." |
| Deepening | Floors 2.2.1-2.2.2 | Dragon-adjacent artifacts in dungeon loot. Environmental clues accumulate. |
| NPC seeds | Floor 1.2, 2.1 | Inn keeper and dispatch veteran hint at "forty years of hero cycles." |
| Climax | Floor 3.1.1 (Ironhold Depths) | Confront the Hero standing over a dying dragon. Three-way choice. |

**Act 1 choice:**

| Option | Immediate Result | Act 2 Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Fight the Hero solo | Hero defeated/killed. Dragon dies. BPRD pleased. | MSS hostile start. Seeker dead or fled. |
| Side with the dragon | Hero driven off. Dragon dies anyway (too late). MSS grateful. | BPRD displeased. Seeker alive and in debt. |
| Attack both | Both wounded. Dragon dies. Chaos. | All factions confused. Seeker status variable. |

The dragon always dies. The Act 1 choice determines faction starting dispositions and whether The Seeker survives into Act 2 as a potential ally. It does NOT lock the player into a faction.

---

### Act 2: The City (GTA 2 / Morrowind pacing)

**Tone:** Dynamic combat. Open city. Faction missions. The player picks a side and makes an enemy. Characters are taught through shared dungeon runs and witnessed behavior, not dialogue trees. The maintenance loop persists but the dungeons are contested.

**Floors:** 3 (Frontier Gate), 3.1-3.4 (faction HQs), 3.1.1+ (Deep Vaults return), 0.1-0.1.2 (Seaway)

**Full design:** See `ACT2_NARRATIVE_OUTLINE.md`

| Phase | Days | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| Open City | 1-8 | Floor 3 unlocks. Four faction HQs. Player runs missions freely. Dispatcher falters. |
| Faction Lock | 9-14 | Player commits to one faction. One opposing faction goes hostile. Surface combat begins. Dispatcher replaced. |
| Seaway Discovery | 15-20 | Ancient lab beneath Floor 0 opens. Mixed ancient/modern dungeon. Original dispatcher found hiding. |
| Climax | 21-25 | Deep Vault return with faction objectives. Seaway Deep confrontation. Hostile faction lead operative boss fight. |

**The Dispatcher Arc:** The player's Act 1 supervisor deteriorates visibly over early Act 2 (behavioral cues, environmental changes, NPC absence). Disappears mid-act. Replaced by a double agent for the hostile faction. Original dispatcher found alive in the Seaway, broken by what they discovered in BPRD's contraband locker. See `ACT2_NARRATIVE_OUTLINE.md` section 5.

**The Seaway:** Beneath Floor 0. A 1624 Jesuit apothecary laboratory repurposed as BPRD's off-books evidence warehouse. Ancient cave wings with anatomical etchings. Sterile modern corridors with caged storage. The environment implies the chimera origin without stating it. See `SEAWAY_FLOOR_DESIGN.md`.

**What Act 2 does NOT reveal:** The Gleaner Protocol (player's own nature), the Pale Court (vampires), the full chimera truth. Act 2 builds attachment and suspicion. Act 3 detonates it.

---

### Act 3: The Truth (design pending)

**Tone:** Betrayals land. The player's faction, their handler, and their own identity are compromised. The conspiracy folds back on the player. Every institution has been using them.

**Floors:** TBD. Likely extends Floor 3 chain and opens a final dungeon beneath the Frontier Gate.

**Key beats (placeholder):** Handler Vala's full agenda revealed. Father Ashworth exposed as architect. The Gleaner Protocol: the player is not fully human. The Pale Court surfaces. Final faction convergence.

## Implementation Notes

1. **Faction state** tracked via `Player.setFlag()` / `Player.getFlag()`. Key flags: `faction_favor_mss`, `faction_favor_pinkerton`, `faction_favor_jesuit`, `faction_favor_bprd` (integers), `faction_locked` (string), `faction_hostile` (string). See `ACT2_NARRATIVE_OUTLINE.md` section 10 for full flag table.
2. **Seeker alive** tracked via `Player.getFlag('seeker_alive')` — set at Act 1 climax based on player choice.
3. **Dispatcher phase** tracked via `Player.getFlag('dispatcher_phase')` — "normal", "faltering", "missing", "replaced".
4. **NPC dialogue trees** registered via `NpcSystem.registerTree(npcId, tree)` in `game.js`. Trees use Morrowind-style branching with `effect` callbacks for flag-setting, currency, and custom actions.
5. **Environmental lore** delivered via peek interactions on bookshelf/chest/wall tiles. No exposition. Object descriptions imply; player infers.
6. **Act transitions** gated by flags: `act2_started` (Floor 3 first entered), `seaway_open` (mid-Act 2 trigger), `seaway_deep_cleared` (Act 2 climax complete).
