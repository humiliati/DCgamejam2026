# Street Chronicles: Dungeon Gleaner — Narrative Outline

> **Operative Gleaner** is a licensed dungeon janitor working for the Gleaner's Guild in the coastal boardwalk town of **Coralshore**. The job: clean up after heroes, restock crates, reset traps, maintain the dungeons. The truth: the dungeons are habitats. The "monsters" are the last of a dying civilization. And Gleaner is an unwitting cog in a machine designed to erase them.

---

## Core Premise

The **Dragon Compact** is 400 years old. It is the founding document of Coralshore — an agreement between the surface settlers and the beings who already lived in the caves below. For four centuries, humans maintained the upper halls; the dragons guarded the deep. The Guild kept the balance.

Now the balance is broken. The **Hero** — a trained operative called **The Seeker** — is systematically hunting dragons in the deep floors. The **Foundry Collective** arms the hero and profits from the kills. The **Admiralty** buys the remains for research. The **Tide Council** launders the artifacts. The **Necromancer** — Gleaner's true employer — created the dungeon reset economy as a cover to keep a few remaining dragons alive in the deepest chambers.

Gleaner doesn't know any of this. Yet.

## The Full Conspiracy Timeline

### 400+ Years Ago — The Founding

The Admiralty sent scholars to the coast of what would become Coralshore. The caves below the cliffs were already inhabited — by beings of great intelligence, longevity, and arcane biology that the Admiralty had studied from a distance for decades. These beings were not "dragons" in the popular sense: they were purpose-bred chimera entities, engineered by an earlier civilization. The Admiralty recognized them immediately.

Rather than study them openly, the Admiralty orchestrated a settlement. They drafted the **Dragon Compact** — a founding agreement that sounded like diplomacy but was designed as a containment and observation protocol. The Gleaner's Guild was founded one year later to maintain "the balance" — actually to maintain the Guild's role as a buffer keeping surface civilians away from the deep chambers.

The Admiralty embedded themselves as the Moonpetal Apothecary: scholarly, benevolent, trusted. Their real work happened in the **Lamplit Catacombs** beneath the building.

### ~80 Years Ago — The Foundry Discovery

The **Foundry Collective** — a private intelligence and arms operation — discovered the Admiralty's secret through industrial espionage. The Foundry realized immediately that there was profit in dragon biology: remains sold to the Admiralty commanded extraordinary prices. Rather than expose the secret and end the trade, the Foundry began manufacturing demand.

They established the "hero trade" — a commercial adventuring industry. Coralshore's first adventuring company was Foundry-sponsored. Hero Licensing was introduced through the Guild (which the Foundry had quietly infiltrated at the Oversight Board level). Within a generation, dragon-slaying was a profession, complete with trade publications, sponsored gear contracts, and recruitment pipelines.

The Foundry sold weapons to heroes entering the dungeons. They bought the recovered dragon remains through front buyers. They sold the remains to the Admiralty through the Tide Council, who took a commission and asked no questions. The system was self-sustaining.

### The Necromancer's Pivot

The **Necromancer** — formerly the Compact's guardian, the being who had negotiated the original agreement with the first Admiralty scholars — discovered the Foundry's infiltration too late to fight it openly. The Foundry had too many connections in too many institutions.

Instead, the Necromancer created the **dungeon reset economy**: a justification to keep Guild operatives cycling through the dungeons, maintaining the appearance of a functioning habitat. The resets served two purposes: they maintained the cover story (dungeons are a resource, not a crime scene), and they let the Necromancer's operatives keep a few remaining dragons alive in the sub-basement chambers that even the hero-contract maps didn't show.

Gleaner is part of this cover. They don't know it.

### Now — The Industrialized Hero Cycle

The hero trade has been running for 80 years. It is so efficient that it generates its own momentum. The Foundry doesn't need to push heroes into dungeons anymore — heroes come voluntarily, chasing glory, sponsored gear contracts, and the social status of dragon kills. The Admiralty buys the remains through the Tide Council. The Guild resets the floors. Everyone profits. The dragons are almost gone.

**The Seeker** is the current cycle's hero. They arrived in Coralshore following Foundry intelligence about "active dragon sites." They may believe they are hunting monsters. Or they may know exactly what they are doing.

### The Admiralty's Schism

Not everyone in the Admiralty is comfortable with what they've become. **Father Ashworth** — one of the inner circle — has concluded that the next hero cycle will kill the last remaining Compact-protected specimens. He advocates releasing the **Providence Codex**: the Admiralty's own internal document proving that dragons are not monsters but sapient protectors under an ancient agreement.

If the Codex is released, the hero trade collapses. The Foundry retaliates. The Tide Council loses its Admiralty contracts. The Guild is exposed as complicit. The Admiralty itself faces scandal.

The mainstream Admiralty wants to maintain the cover-up. Ashworth is outvoted but not silenced.

**Dr. Yuen** — the Admiralty researcher who independently discovered what the Catacombs actually were — disappeared after leaving fragmentary notes. Her last known documents are hidden in the deep floors.

## Story Arcs

### Act 1 — "The Gleaner's First Week" (Jam Scope)

**Setting**: Floors "1" through "2.2.2"

**Beat 1 — Arrival**: Gleaner arrives in Coralshore. Meets the Dispatcher. Receives first work order. The dungeons are a mess — heroes have no respect for the workspace. Business as usual.

**Beat 2 — First Anomaly**: On the first restock run, Gleaner finds things that don't belong in a "monster lair": sophisticated construction, equipment caches, signs of long habitation. The Dispatcher's answer is boilerplate: "Dungeons are complex environments. Stay on task."

**Beat 3 — The Seeker**: Gleaner encounters The Seeker in the field — or finds evidence of their recent passage. The Seeker's focus is specific: not loot, not glory. Dragon locations. Gleaner notes this in their field journal.

**Beat 4 — The Moonpetal Visit**: A sealed crate from Floor B2 arrives at the Moonpetal Apothecary. Gleaner witnesses the handoff. The Tide Council vendor asks Gleaner if they've found anything "unusual." The Guild memo on foreign investigators reads differently after this.

**Beat 5 — The Deep Find**: In Hero's Wake B2, Gleaner finds one of Dr. Yuen's research notes — or Father Ashworth's letter. The language is explicit enough to understand that the "monsters" are not what the Guild calls them. The work orders are not innocent.

**Beat 6 — Choice Point** (end of Act 1): Gleaner must decide how to file their next report. Three options:
1. **File standard report** — stay in the system, remain useful to the Necromancer's cover
2. **Report the anomaly to the Guild** — triggers Oversight Board attention; accelerates the Foundry's interest in Gleaner
3. **Contact Handler Vala** — if Gleaner found the cipher; opens the Act 2 conspiracy thread

## Breadcrumb Revelation Order

The conspiracy is revealed through accumulation. No single document states the whole truth. The order below tracks when the player should encounter each breadcrumb.

### Phase 1 — "Something Is Off" (Floors "1", "1.1", "1.2", "1.3")
- `lore_coralshore_founding`: Town was founded on "pre-existing caves" — already occupied
- `lore_gleaner_guild_charter`: Guild founded to serve the *Compact*, not commerce
- `notice_curfew_policy`: Standard regulation, slightly unusual precision
- `journal_operative_brief`: Gleaner is a "Tier 4 Civic Contractor" serving "interests of the Compact" — who owns the Compact?
- `journal_contract_terms`: NDA clause: "Do not disclose the contents of chambers below depth 2.0" — strange for a janitor

### Phase 2 — "The Trade Is Bigger Than It Looks" (Floors "1.6", "2", "2.1", "2.2")
- `lore_adventuring_economy`: Foundry expansion correlates with increased dragon sighting reports
- `lore_history_of_heroes`: First adventuring company was Foundry-sponsored; coincidence noted
- `notice_tide_council_artifact_prices`: Admiralty contract items at top of price list
- `notice_hero_registration`: Foundry Collective is an approved hero sponsor; three current heroes are Foundry-sponsored
- `notice_guild_foreign_investigators`: Standard narrative is a *narrative* — something to recite
- `journal_field_notes_week1`: Gleaner (self-authored) notices the Admiralty crate, the Tide Council vendor's question

### Phase 3 — "Something Is Being Hidden" (Floor "2.2.1", "2.2.2")
- `letter_foundry_field_report`: "Keep the Gleaner uninformed"; specimen harvest window
- `letter_guild_memo_redacted`: The redactions ARE the conspiracy
- `lore_dragon_naturalis`: Dragons are sapient; paper was REDACTED by the Admiralty
- `letter_seeker_journal_fragment`: The Seeker is figuring it out. "The janitor keeps appearing before us."
- `letter_agent_crow_report`: Crow is tracking Gleaner specifically; something is alive in the sub-basement

### Phase 4 — "The Full Picture" (Deepest Floors — Act 1 Climax)

- `letter_dr_yuen_notes`: "The Catacombs are a slaughterhouse with academic credentials"

## Tone Notes

**The game is not nihilistic.** The Necromancer is genuinely trying to protect something. The Dispatcher is genuinely trying to do their job. The Seeker may be a victim of the same system as Gleaner. Even the Tide Council knows they've made a moral compromise.

**Gleaner's voice is dry, professional, slightly smug.** They entered Coralshore thinking this was a simple gig. The field journals should read as a competent operative slowly realizing they have been placed on the wrong side of a much bigger game.

**The conspiracy is a tragedy, not a thriller.** The dragons are not helpless — they are ancient, sapient, and patient. But they are also almost gone. The urgency is real. The hero cycle is not evil by intention; it is evil by design — a machine that has outlasted the intentions of its builders.

**Writing register**: In-world documents should sound like they were written by people who live in this world without self-consciousness. Academic texts are dry. Guild memos are procedural. Personal letters are human. Hero-trade publications are cheerfully mercenary. The conspiracy is revealed by reading between the lines of texts that were never meant to be read together.

**No fourth-wall breaks. No meta-commentary. No genre awareness.** Coralshore is a real place. The Compact is a real legal document. The dragons are real beings. The trade is real commerce. The horror is that it all makes sense from inside the world.
