### Structural Intent

**1. Compression → Release**
- Floor2 ends in ceremony → **tight boardwalk entry**
- Immediate **tree break** (`#####-----#####`) = threshold moment
- Player spills into **open, low-density wilds**

**2. Campground Echo (Floor0 callback)**
- Central **bonfire clearing**
- One **functional shack** (supplies / save / narrative anchor)
- Familiar layout language, but now **degraded and expanded**

**3. Implied Scale**
- Repeating shack pattern extends laterally → suggests **much larger unseen sprawl**
- Outer `~` = unbounded wild terrain (not fully explorable yet, but readable)

---

### Population Design

**NPCs (very sparse)**
- Roles:
  - scavenger (short loops between shacks)
  - watch (slow perimeter patrols)
  - caretaker (camp-bound, near bonfire)
- Behavior:
  - low urgency, long idle states
  - minimal cross-traffic → emphasizes emptiness

**Enemies (very few)**
- `x` = roaming threats
- Wide spacing, unpredictable paths
- Designed to feel like **intrusions, not inhabitants**

---

### Pathing Topology

- Boardwalk → **single vector in**
- Camp clearing → **soft hub**
- Shack rows → **branching but shallow**
- Locked gate → **clear long-term goal anchor**

No tight loops — movement is **loose, breathable**

---

### The Locked Gate (Floor4)

- Visually present early but **distant**
- Should read as:
  - heavy
  - intentional
  - not solvable “yet”
- Encourages:
  - exploration of wilds
  - return trips
  - narrative buildup

---

### Tone Shift (critical)

- Floor1: busy, civic  
- Floor2: ceremonial, directed  
- **Floor3: quiet, uncertain, expansive**

You want the player to feel:
> “I’m no longer in a system — I’m at the edge of one.”
