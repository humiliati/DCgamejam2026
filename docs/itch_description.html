Every sealed crypt has fresh vegetables.

Every "ancient" chest is fully stocked with health potions. Every cobweb is exactly one sword-swing old. Every trap resets between adventuring parties like clockwork.

Someone is doing this. Someone has always been doing this.

You are that someone.

Dungeon Gleaner is a first-person dungeon crawler about the other side of the hero economy. You are Operative Gleaner — a licensed dungeon maintenance contractor dispatched to a small coastal boardwalk town. While the heroes kick down doors, loot every crate, and leave scorch marks on the ceiling, you come in after. Scrub the walls. Restock the cheese wheels. Re-arm the traps. File your readiness report. Clock out.

Your combat deck isn't found in treasure chests. It's earned crate by crate, mop by mop.

♣ ♦ ♠ ♥   Three Loops

Clean. Every surface has a condition. Scrub tiles for coin. Work your way up from a damp rag to a pressure washer. The Magic Remote pointer aims your cleaning — wax on, wax off.

Restock. Crates have color-coded slots that hint at what goes where. Fill them with dungeon junk for a coin each, or buy the right supplies from faction shops for double the payout. Seal a crate perfectly and it might drop a combat card.

Reset. The Gleaner's Guild posts work orders: hit a readiness target before the next hero cycle. Clean tiles, fill crates, re-arm traps, scramble puzzles, restore secrets. Heroes arrive on schedule to undo everything you just did. Circle of commerce.

◢   The Town

A retrofuturistic coastal boardwalk. A civilization that discovered magic before electricity — chrome railings bolted onto marble promenades, neon sigils buzzing over old timber shop fronts, vaporwave sunsets bleeding across stone walls.

Three districts above. Building interiors one floor down — the Coral Bazaar, the Gleaner's Guild hall, the Driftwood Inn, the Watchman's post. Dungeons beneath those.

The deeper you go, the quieter it gets.

▼   The Conspiracy

The heroes aren't clearing monsters. They're hunting dragons.

Not the fire-breathing village-burners from the storybooks. Ancient protectors. Guardians who've held a four-hundred-year compact with the town's founders. Every dungeon floor hides another piece of it — unsigned letters, redacted field bulletins, a torn journal page that reads:

"...the heroes do not know what sleeps below."

Three factions compete for what the dragons leave behind.

  • The Tide Council — hoards relics, whispers about the old Compact.
  • The Foundry — melts everything down, asks no questions.
  • The Admiralty — classifies, catalogs, and denies.

Your Dispatcher is honest and uninformed. These are not the same thing.

♠   Combat

Suit-triangle card combat. Clubs beat Diamonds beat Spades beat Clubs. Hearts break the rules.

You don't find your deck in loot drops. You build it through labor — sealed crates, faction reputation, shop purchases. Every card you play was earned with a mop in your hand first. Swipe and drop. Pair cards for synergies. The Magic Remote gyro is central to targeting.

◈   Wax On, Wax Off

The cleaning tools teach you to fight.

The cargo sling that adds bag slots also builds the frame that absorbs a punch. The loot magnet that pulls coins to you also trains projectile awareness. The foreman's harness that distributes weight also centers your balance.

Labor is training. The dungeon knows it even if the Guild doesn't.
